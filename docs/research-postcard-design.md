# Research: postcard design & copy for plumber/HVAC owner-operators — 2026-07-13

Deep-research run (101 agents, 5 search angles, 3-vote adversarial verification per
claim). 11 claims confirmed 3-0; 13 pieces of common direct-mail lore refuted as
unverifiable against primary sources. This doc drives the wave-1 creative bets.

## Confirmed (3-0 verification)

1. **Loss framing beats gain framing** — experiment (RIPS/IRSP, cholesterol-screening
   intentions): loss-framed M=3.48 vs non-gain M=2.65 vs gain M=2.49; framing ×
   personal-relevance interaction t(222)=4.79, p=.001. A *loss* (someone took the job)
   is felt harder than a *non-gain* (you could book more).
   https://rips-irsp.com/articles/10.5334/irsp.15
2. **Response-efficacy mediates the loss-frame advantage** (ab=0.16, CI [0.09,0.25]) —
   the loss message converts only if the reader believes the fix works. Implication:
   make the demo provable ON the card ("call this number, watch what happens").
3. **Type for 50+ readers: 14pt body recommended, 12pt minimum**, generous leading
   (DC.gov age-friendly guide; marketing-partners.com typography-for-aging). Caveat
   (PMC 9376262): legibility gains stop at a critical size — huge ≠ better forever.
4. **QR/URL is the dominant response-*tracking* mechanism** (ANA 2023: 82% of
   trackers, up from 67%; phone tracking 39%) — a marketer-behavior stat, NOT
   evidence the trades audience prefers QR. Phone/text prominence stays our bet.
5. **Benchmarks (ANA 2023, small self-reported samples)**: cold/prospect lists 10.8%
   avg response, $97.74 cost/conversion, 33.7% ROI; house lists 15.6% / $24.48 /
   160.9%. B2B CPA across media avg **$341.90**. The primary source explicitly CANNOT
   break out B2B-postcard-to-cold-list (insufficient N) — every vendor number at that
   granularity is unverifiable.
   https://theworldsgreatestmarketing.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/rr-2024-02-ana-response-rate-report-2023.pdf

## Refuted / unverifiable lore (do not cite in decisions)

- "4.4% DM vs 0.12% email" vendor stat; "prospect lists 2.0–4.4%, house 5–9%";
  "B2B 5.3%/2.9% per ANA 2023" — none survive against the primary report.
- "Oversized postcards add ~half a point of response over standard" — unverified.
  (6x9 decision stands on CONFIRMED ground instead: 14pt+ body + dominant phone
  number don't fit a 4x6; decisions.md 2026-07-12 rationale amended by this note.)
- Specific multi-touch lift numbers ("3 drops = 2–3x", "touches 2–3 add 20–40%") —
  no primary support found. Cadence itself isn't refuted; the numbers are folklore.
- "QR-to-SMS converts 3–5x callbacks"; "81% landing-page abandonment" — vendor
  marketing.
- "Letters beat postcards for cold B2B lists" — refuted as stated.
- "Headlines must be ≤7 words, benefit-framed" — vendor lore; the framing evidence
  points the other way (loss > benefit).

## The wave-1 creative bets (variants minted 2026-07-13)

| Variant | Bet | Grounded in |
|---|---|---|
| `w1 6x9 loss-math` | Dollar-quantified loss ("that missed call was a $400 job… went to whoever picked up") | #1 — loss > non-gain > gain |
| `w1 6x9 demo-first` | The live demo IS the headline ("Call this number. We won't pick up. Watch what happens next.") | #2 — response-efficacy mediates |
| `w1 6x9 jobsite-voice` | Blameless identity moment ("You're under a slab… that was today's best job") | #1's relevance interaction |
| `ghost 6x9 a1/a2` (2026-07-12) | Control pair: generic pain vs benefit | pre-research baseline |

Shared mechanics on every card: 18pt body / 1.5 leading, 30pt call-or-text line,
`getnevermisscall.com/?r={{mailer_code}}`, sample-safe code footer. No QR yet (no
per-piece QR generation in the pipeline; the printed code/URL carries attribution).

## Expectation-setting

Plan wave 1 against single-digit response and up to ~$342 CPA as the B2B norm; at
$200–300/mo subscription, unit economics tolerate that. Grade variants on
cost-per-response via the wave readout, not against vendor benchmark folklore.
